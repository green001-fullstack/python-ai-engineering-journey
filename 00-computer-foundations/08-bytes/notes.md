# FACTS

A bit is one binary digit.
A byte is eight bits.
A byte has 256 possible combinations.

# IDEAS

The same byte can represent different things depending on how it is interpreted.
A byte is not a character—it is just a container of 8 bits.


When we write:

name = "Emmanuel"

you won't just see a string.

You'll know that:

the string is made of characters,
the characters are encoded into bytes,
the bytes are made of bits,
the bits represent electrical states,
and those states ultimately come from billions of transistors switching on and off.
